Abstract
To show that intrauterine anesthesia is a reliable method for reducing pain associated with endocavitary procedures.A prospective, randomized, double-blind study.The Department of Obstetrics and Gynecology, Tor Vergata University of Rome, Rome, Italy.Forty-five patients undergoing diagnostic hysteroscopy (n = 27) or hysteroscopy and endometrial biopsy (n = 18).Five milliliters of 2% mepivacaine or saline solution were injected transcervically into the uterine cavity before performing the procedures.Evaluation of pain reduction on a visual analogue scale.Pain expectation and pain reported were reduced during and after the procedures.Topical anesthesia effectively reduces pain during hysteroscopy and endometrial biopsy.
